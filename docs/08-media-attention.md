# 08 — Media attention & AI positioning

## Goal

Turn the **AI-party novelty** into a **truth + waste** brand — then into votes among net taxpayers. Attention without conversion is a TED talk.

## The trap

| Trap | Symptom | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Clown | “Robot party lol” segments only | Always pay the tax: 4 facts per interview |
| Cult | Founder = AI prophet | Rotate faces; publish methods |
| Rage bait | Algorithm rewards cruelty | Mechanisms > insults; still be sharp |
| Elite only | De Tijd yes, doorstep no | Dual track: serious + simple |

## The 4 facts (memorise)

Every interview, podcast, debate:

1. **Labour tax wedge** — Belgium top of OECD class (~52%+ for average single worker)  
2. **Deficit/debt** — structural gap, debt >100% GDP order of magnitude  
3. **One leaderboard item** — concrete absurd subsidy/rule  
4. **One fix** — bill-shaped, not vibes  

If time for only one sentence: *“We tax work like enemies and subsidise almost everything — then wonder why the productive leave.”*

### Pillars

### Platforms (Flanders-first)

- **X** — speed + elite discourse  
- **YouTube / podcasts** — depth  
- **LinkedIn** — SME owners  
- **TikTok/Reels** — 30s wedge explainers (human face on camera)  
- **Classic press** — De Tijd, Trends, HLN, VRT interviews when earned  

Do not buy illegal or regulated-period ads by accident. Know *sperperiode* rules.

## AI as product, not god

### Good uses (public)

- Open-source policy memos with assumptions listed  
- Interactive budget explorer  
- “Simulate this subsidy” notebooks  
- Multilingual explainers  
- Debate prep against all parties  

### Bad uses (public)

- Fake candidate deepfakes  
- Automated mass replies that break platform/law  
- Claiming AI “decides” votes of MPs  
- Unreviewed legal or medical advice  

### Brand sentence

> **Humans accountable. Machines for truth-checking. Incentives for abundance.**

---

## Launch sequence (attention rocket)

1. **Leak-friendly asset:** top 10 absurdities one-pager  
2. **Founding essay:** “Belgium’s incentive problem” in a serious outlet  
3. **Live stream:** first DOGE ranking with data people  
4. **Challenge:** invite every party to accept/reject each cut on camera  
5. **Signature of the week:** show organising is real, not only Twitter  

### Earned media hooks that work

- “AI-augmented party” (novelty)  
- “We’ll publish every assumption” (contrast)  
- “Highest tax wedge + deficit — show the math” (pocketbook)  
- “Subsidy leaderboard” (shareable)  
- “We’ll resign if compact broken” (integrity drama)

### Hooks that backfire

- Ethnic collective blame  
- Apocalypse cosplay without data  
- Claiming majority support you don’t have  
- Insulting journalists as a personality brand
