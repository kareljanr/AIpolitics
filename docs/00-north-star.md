# 00 — North star

## One sentence

**Seek the truth about what works; implement the policies that create the most sustainable abundance for the most people — including those who need support — without destroying the productive base that makes support possible.**

## Why “truth”

Politics usually optimises for:

- coalition survival
- media narrative
- clienteles and transfers
- moral fashion

We optimise for **accurate models of cause and effect**. Fashion is allowed only as marketing packaging for true claims — never as a substitute for them.

### Operational definition of truth-seeking

1. State the claim clearly.
2. State the confidence (Strong / Medium / Weak / Speculative).
3. Name the mechanism (incentives, constraints, selection effects).
4. Name what would **falsify** the claim.
5. Update or kill the claim when falsified.

If a sacred party line fails the test, the line dies — not the evidence.

## Abundance, not theatre

**Abundance** means more real goods and options over time:

- higher real wages and productivity
- more housing per person
- cheaper reliable energy
- shorter waiting times in core public services
- more firms, talent, and experimentation

**Not abundance:**

- redistributing a shrinking pie with better slogans
- “free” services funded by debt and stealth inflation
- regulations that protect insiders while claiming to protect citizens

## The transfer constraint (non-negotiable)

You asked for a safety net **without** destroying the productive group.

Hard constraint:

> Net transfers from productive → non-productive populations are limited by **exit, entry, and fertility of the productive base**.

If the tax + regulation + cultural package makes high producers leave (or never arrive), and low-contribution inflows grow, the system does not become “more social” — it becomes **poorer and more conflictual**.

Implications:

| Do | Don't |
|----|--------|
| Clear, time-bound support for people who cannot compete | Open-ended subsidies that reward non-work |
| Conditionality where legal (language, job search, training) | Pretend incentives don't matter |
| Attract superproductive talent | Punish success as moral failure |
| Measure employment, crime, fiscal net contribution | Measure only spending and “rights delivered” |

This is not cruelty. It is the only way a welfare state survives contact with reality.

## Default ideology (revisable under evidence)

**Working priors** (high confidence they beat Belgian status quo; always open to falsification):

1. **Markets + property rights** allocate most scarce goods better than ministries.
2. **Simple, lower labour taxation** raises employment and reduces black economy.
3. **Housing abundance** (YIMBY) beats rent control theatre.
4. **Energy abundance** beats performative scarcity.
5. **Rule of law + safety** is a precondition for freedom, not the opposite of it.
6. **Deregulation of entry** (firms, professions, building) beats industrial policy cosplay.
7. **Selective migration** oriented to contribution beats both open-door and pure ethno-politics as economic strategy.
8. **DOGE-style spending cuts** on low-value programmes beat “efficiency commissions” that never cut.

When evidence shows a market failure *and* a state intervention that actually fixes it cheaply, take it. When the state fails the burden of proof: **no policy**.

## Red lines

- No violence, no illegal funding, no fake democracy cosplay.
- No lying about numbers to win a cycle.
- No coalition deal that locks in structural deficit growth for a ministry title.
- No cult of the founder or the AI — rotate spokespeople; publish models.

## Success metrics (civilisation level)

Track these, not applause:

1. Employment rate (especially low-skilled and prime-age)
2. Labour tax wedge vs OECD peers
3. Primary budget balance path
4. Housing permits and real rents
5. PISA / skills + share of STEM graduates employed productively
6. Net fiscal contribution by cohort (where data allows)
7. Business formation and bankruptcy time
8. Violent crime and street safety perception (with honest stats)

## Relationship to “AI party”

AI is a **method and a brand**, not a theology:

- Method: force public debate onto numbers, simulations, open ledgers.
- Brand: “we check assumptions; legacy parties perform empathy.”
- Risk: gimmick → ignore substance. Mitigation: weekly **waste leaderboard** + falsified policies killed in public.
