## 0. Meta: how we govern

1. **Open fiscal dashboard** for any level we control — line items, not PDFs.  
2. **DOGE office** with public kill-list and quarterly scorecard ([06-doge-belgium.md](06-doge-belgium.md)).  
3. **Regulatory budget** — ministries cannot add net burden without cuts elsewhere.  
4. **Sunset clauses** on new spending programmes (default 5 years).  
5. **AI transparency** — models and assumptions for major forecasts published.

## 1. Work & taxes (core)

### Goals

- Cut the **labour tax wedge** toward OECD average over a legislated path.  
- Make full-time legal work always beat long-term benefit dependency for people who can work.  
- Simplify: fewer special regimes, broader base, lower rates.

### Draft measures

| Measure | Level | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Multi-year wedge reduction law (employee + employer SSC redesign) | Federal | Phased; pair with spending cuts |
| Flatten benefit cliffs (smooth phase-outs) | Federal / region | Kill poverty traps |
| Shift where possible from labour tax toward less mobile bases / pollution prices | Federal | Not a VAT explosion without wage relief |
| Tax expenditure cull (company cars, niche deductions with bad ROI) | Federal | See DOGE list |
| Faster VAT refunds / less admin for SMEs | Federal | Cash-flow is oxygen |

**Falsifier:** employment rate and hours worked for low/mid skills do not improve within 5 years of major cut *and* dynamic revenue collapses beyond model band without external shock.

### What we will not do

- Promise Danish services with Singapore taxes without saying what dies.  
- “Tax the rich” as sole plan while the middle is already crushed.

## 2. Safety net (incentive-compatible)

### Principle

Support people who **cannot** fully participate. Do not build a lifestyle for people who **will not**.

### Draft measures

- **Clear tiers:** disability / inability vs temporary unemployment vs unwillingness.  
- **Activation with teeth** where legal: job search, training, community work options.  
- **Anti-fraud** as social justice for actual contributors.  
- **In-kind + time-limited** cash for work-capable adults.  
- Protect a **floor** against destitution; refuse an **open-ended relative** lifestyle race funded by exit-prone taxpayers.

**Falsifier:** child poverty rises *and* employment does not, after reforms — reassess design (not automatically “spend more forever”).

---

## 3. Migration & citizenship (contribution-first)

### Principle

Belgium may choose who enters long-term settlement. Rules should maximise contribution and minimise parallel societies.

### Draft measures

- Economic migration scored on skills/language/job offer.  
- Asylum: fast procedures; remove failed applicants; humane but firm.  
- Access to long-term non-contributory benefits tied to legal status + residence conditions (within EU/Belgian law).  
- Language + work requirements for lasting integration tracks.  
- Zero tolerance for imported political violence and organised clan crime.  
- Attract **superproductive** talent aggressively (fast track, housing help via market supply — not more bureaucracy).

**Hard line on rhetoric:** critique **rules and outcomes**, not blood. Collective ethnic guilt is false and strategically suicidal.

**Falsifier:** cohort net fiscal contribution and employment for non-EU arrivals under new rules fail to beat baseline within a decade.

## 4. Pensions

- Align career length with longevity (automatic adjusters).  
- Close early-exit highways that dump costs on workers.  
- Strengthen second/third pillar with portable, simple products.  
- Honesty: no “everything stays free and earlier.”

---

## 5. Housing (abundance)

- YIMBY: upzone transit corridors; by-right mid-rise where infrastructure allows.  
- Permit clocks with consequences.  
- Prefer supply over permanent rent freezes.  
- Public land: lease for middle housing, not only deep social.  
- Student and starter housing via conversion of dead offices.

Local proof → Flemish code reforms.

---

## 6. Energy & climate (abundance + price signals)

- Reliability and price first; CO₂ via prices/innovation, not romantic scarcity.  
- Nuclear + grids + permitting reform for renewables where cost-effective.  
- Stop subsidy stacking that enriches intermediaries without tonnes reduced.  
- Industry: stay competitive or watch factories (and emissions) export.

---

## 7. Safety & justice

- Data-driven policing; prosecute street violence and drug retail hard.  
- Court capacity: time-to-judgment as a KPI.  
- No romanticism about “youth crime” when patterns are chronic.  
- Free speech protected; violence and intimidation not.

Freedom dies in unsafe streets. Order is liberal infrastructure.

---

## 8. Education & skills

- Focus on literacy, numeracy, STEM, Dutch (and French/English as assets).  
- Vocational prestige equal to academic tracks.  
- School choice + transparency of outcomes.  
- Universities: excellence and labour-market feedback, not infinite administrative bloating.

---

## 9. Health

- Outcome transparency (waiting times, quality).  
- Contestability where monopoly kills.  
- Prevention via incentives, not only campaigns.  
- Fight waste and fraud in billing.  
- Do not pretend “extra health taxes” on top of the highest labour wedge are free.

---

## 10. State structure & EU

- Support **whatever competence split** improves accountability and fiscal hard budgets — not identity theology.  
- Flemish autonomy where it improves labour/education outcomes.  
- EU: complete single market, capital markets; resist ever-expanding soft-law that raises energy and housing costs without votes.  
- Brussels: pragmatic functionality; no hobby hatred.

---

## 11. Local (beachhead programme)

For any commune we contest:

1. Publish full budget in machine-readable form  
2. Cut low-value subsidies and vanity projects first  
3. Speed permits  
4. Clean & safe public space SLAs  
5. Business desk that answers in 48h  

Local competence is the ad for regional power.

---

## Priority stack for first 100 days in executive power

1. Install DOGE + open budget  
2. Freeze new non-essential spending  
3. Table labour tax path + paired cut list  
4. Emergency permitting reform for housing/energy  
5. Migration enforcement package within legal competence  
6. Publish coalition compact compliance dashboard
