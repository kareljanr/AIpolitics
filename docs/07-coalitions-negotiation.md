# 07 — Coalitions & negotiation

Belgium is a **coalition country**. Ideal pure power is rare. The skill is: **extract structural reform without becoming decoration**.

## Iron law

> Never trade a **structural fiscal commitment** for a **symbolic ministry**.

Titles fade. Tax wedges and spending baselines compound.

---

## Pre-conditions to enter talks

You only open coalition talks if:

1. You have seats (or a formal cartel with seats)  
2. Your **compact v1** is published *before* horse-trading (anchors the Overton window)  
3. Your members understand second-best vs betrayal  

If partners refuse to put numbers in writing, there is no negotiation — only absorption.

---

## The compact (must-have basket)

Ranked. Lose low ranks before high ranks.

### Tier S — walk-away if missing

| Item | Metric |
|------|--------|
| Multi-year **primary balance path** | Written €/GDP targets |
| **Labour tax wedge** reduction schedule | OECD-comparable path |
| **DOGE cut list** with euro targets | Year-1 and year-3 |
| No new unfunded permanent entitlements | Hard rule |
| Migration/contribution enforcement package | Within competence |

### Tier A — fight hard

| Item | Metric |
|------|--------|
| Housing permitting reform | Statutory clocks + upzoning |
| Regulatory one-in-two-out | Decree/circular |
| Pension automatic adjusters | Law |
| Open budget law | Machine-readable |

### Tier B — tradable

- Specific cultural dossiers  
- Secondary offices  
- Local pork that doesn’t break Tier S  
- EU theatre wording  

### Tier F — never

- Secret side letters that reverse Tier S  
- Accepting “study commissions” as substitute for cuts  
- Gagging the leaderboard  

---

## Negotiation tactics

### 1. Publish first

Release compact + DOGE list **publicly** the day talks start. Partners who dilute you face media arithmetic.

### 2. Score every counter-offer

```text
Δ wedge, Δ primary balance, Δ housing permits, Δ net migration rules
```

If the vector is ~0, reject politely and leak the scorecard.

### 3. Prefer finance + reform portfolios

Good: Budget, Finance, Work, Economy, Interior (asylum), Administrative simplification.  
Vanity: purely ceremonial digital glory without budget power.

### 4. Use time as leverage

Belgian formations are long. Your threat point is **not** chaos for its own sake — it is a credible alternative majority or a long opposition that grows.

### 5. Minority support deals

Sometimes: external support for specific reform bills without full cabinet. Cleaner brand; less capture. Use when Tier S is available à la carte.

### 6. Document everything

Versioned offers. Members see the same text. Reduces founder-only mystique and betrayal narratives.

---

## In government: compliance

1. Public **compact dashboard** updated monthly  
2. Walk-away clause: if Tier S breached, resign and publish why  
3. DOGE quarterly hearing  
4. No “temporary” emergency spending without sunset  

Resignation is a feature. Fear of opposition is how parties become mush.

---

## In opposition: still power

If locked out:

- Parliamentary questions feed leaderboard  
- Alternative budget each year  
- Train shadow ministers  
- Local executive beachheads implement micro-DOGE  

Opposition with a clearer fiscal story than government is how you win the next median voter.
