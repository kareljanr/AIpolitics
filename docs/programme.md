# Policy programme (draft)

Priorities for **Ghent** first. Flemish and federal positions follow from local proof.

---

## 1. Economy & entrepreneurship

### Ghent as startup and scale-up hub

- **One-stop shop** for starting a business: KBO, VAT, social security, and city permits in a single digital flow (< 48h target for standard cases)
- **Innovation zone** around Tech Lane, Bio-Incubator, and port logistics — streamlined zoning, not more committees
- **Talent visa support** at city level: help international hires with housing and integration (complement federal rules)

### Tax & fees

- Freeze and review **city surcharges** on top of Flemish/regional taxes — publish a "tax map" per neighbourhood
- **Parking and mobility pricing** based on congestion data, not revenue maximisation
- Reduce **event and terrace permit friction** for hospitality — standardised packages for Sint-Pietersplein, Vrijdagmarkt, etc.

### Labour

- Support **flexible work** arrangements in city contracts and procurement
- Apprenticeship partnerships with UGent, HoGent, and local employers — target sectors with shortages (care, tech, construction)

---

## 2. Housing

Ghent's housing crisis needs supply and speed, not only rent controls.

- **YIMBY zoning**: automatic upzoning near tram/bus corridors and station areas
- **Permit clock**: statutory maximum processing time with automatic approval if breached (with safety checks)
- **Public land**: leasehold models for mid-market housing, not only social rent
- **Student housing**: convert underused office stock; stop pretending students aren't part of the housing market

---

## 3. Mobility

Liberal mobility = priced scarcity + alternatives, not car hatred or car worship.

- **Congestion pricing** for inner ring during peak hours — revenue to trams and cycle infrastructure
- **Tram priority** on conflict corridors; data-driven light timing
- **Secure bike parking** at Sint-Pieters, Dampoort, and major employers
- **Port and industry logistics**: night windows and routing tech, not blanket bans

---

## 4. Smart government

- **Open budget dashboard** — ward-level spending visible to citizens
- **API-first** city services; no PDF-only forms in 2026
- **AI for routine queries** (permits, waste, leisurePath)
- **Performance contracts** for city agencies with published KPIs
- **Merge overlapping advisory bodies** — Ghent has too many talking shops

---

## 5. Sustainability (market-compatible)

Climate policy via **price signals and innovation**, not prohibition by default.

- **Carbon-aware procurement** for city contracts
- **District heating** where economics work; don't force where they don't
- **Building renovation**: simplify subsidies, pay for outcomes (kWh saved), not paperwork
- **Green port** investments as economic strategy, not virtue signal

---

## 6. Safety & public space

- **Data-driven policing** — hotspot deployment, not random visibility
- **Zero tolerance for violence**; support victims and swift prosecution
- **Clean streets** as basic competence: waste, graffiti response SLAs
- **Event economy** — balance nightlife and residential life with clear rules, not ad hoc politics

---

## 7. Education & integration

- **Dual-language excellence** in technical education — Ghent's economy is global
- **Bridge programmes** from school to work for youth not on academic track
- **Citizenship = participation** — language, work or study, respect for rule of law; benefits tied to contribution where legally possible

---

## 8. Europe & federal context

- **Pro-EU, pro-reform** — complete the single market, digitalise cross-border services
- **Flemish autonomy** on labour market and education where it improves outcomes — not as identity project
- **Brussels relationship** — pragmatic, not hostile; Ghent benefits from a functional capital

---

## How we decide

Every policy above is **draft**. We adopt positions when:

1. Evidence supports them
2. They are fiscally honest
3. A local majority of members agrees

Disagreement is welcome. Groupthink is not.
